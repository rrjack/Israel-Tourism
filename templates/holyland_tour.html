Day 2
Amman, capital of Jordan

Jordan officially the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, is an Arab country in Western Asia, on the East Bank of the Jordan River. Jordan is bordered by Saudi Arabia to the south and the east, Iraq to the north-east, Syria to the north and Israel and Palestine (West Bank) to the west.

Day 3
Cairo, capital of Egypt

Egypt, where israelites were slave and Moses leads them out of Egypt through the wilderness to Mount Sinai, where Yahweh reveals himself to his people and gave Moses the ten commandments. Egypt is a Mediterranean country bordered by the Gaza Strip and Israel to the northeast, the Gulf of Aqaba and the Red Sea to the east, Sudan to the south, and Libya to the west.

Day 4
The Great Pyramid of Giza in Egypt

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex bordering present-day El Giza, Egypt. It is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only one to remain largely intact.

Day 5
The Nile river in Egypt

The Nile, which is about 6,650 km (4,130 mi) long, is an "international" river as its drainage basin covers eleven countries, namely, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Kenya, Ethiopia, Eritrea, South Sudan, Republic of the Sudan and Egypt. In particular, the Nile is the primary water source of Egypt and Sudan.

Day 6
Jerusalem, capital of Israel

Jerusalem, the city of David, is considered as the holiest land in the world and certainly one of the best places to visit in Israel. Popular tourist attractions in Jerusalem are The Western Wall, Mar Saba Monastery, Visit Yad Vashem, Temple Mount and Dome of Rocks, City of David and Hezekiah’s Tunnel, and St. George’s Monastery.

Day 7
Bethlehem

The birthplace of Jesus is certainly one of the best places to visit in Israel. Popular tourist attractions in Bethlehem are Shepherd’s Field, Church of the Nativity, St. Catherine’s Church, Manger Square, Old Bethlehem Museum, and Herodium Hill. Herodium Hill – almost 10 km from Jerusalem. Mount of Olives lies to the north of the Herodium and Dead Sea in the east.

Day 8
Nazareth

Nazareth, the village of Jesus, the city in Israel with biblical history where the angel Gabriel told Mary she would bear a child.. Nazareth town has been known as the place where Jesus spent his adolescent days. Popular tourist attractions in Nazareth are Church of Annunciation, Megiddo National Park, St. Joseph Church, St. Gabriel’s Church, Mt. of Precipice Lookout Point, and Holy Caves & Ancient Bath House of Nazareth.

Day 9
Tel Aviv

coastal city has some of the most happening beaches in Israel, such as Gordon Beach, Frishman Beach, and Banana Beach. With an extended coastline, Tel Aviv offers a lot of water activities and access to its rich marine parks. Popular tourist attractions in Tel Aviv are Yarkon River and Park Hayarkon, Carmel Market for Flea & street market, Dizengoff Circle and Surrounds, Tel Aviv Museum of Art, and Bialik Street.

Day 10
Haifa

The port city, owing to its strategic location, is a massive lure for tourists. Gardens, beaches, promenades, and monasteries adorn the city that has been outlined by a steep cliff and a perfect landscape. Popular tourist attractions in Haifa are Marvel at the Baha’i Gardens, Mount Carmel, Tel Megiddo – ancient site, Carmel National Park, Elijah’s Cave, and Downtown Haifa.

Day 11
Jaffa

The city has been mentioned in the holy Bible to be named after Noah’s son Japhet. Popular tourist attractions in Jaffa are Jaffa Port, Jaffa Slope Park, Clock Tower, HaPisgah Gardens, Great Mosque, Ilana Goor Museum, and music & entertainment shows at flea market area every Thursday night.

Day 12
Dead Sea

Located almost 400 m below the sea level in the heart of Israel, Dead Sea is nature’s true wonder. It is mentioned in the bible many times where Ezekiel's prophecy fulfilled about Dead sea, for more, read ezekiel chapter 47. Popular tourist attractions near the Dead Sea are Masada Mountains & fortress – archeological wonder, Mujib Nature Reserve, Kerak Castle, Dead Sea Museum, Lot’s Cave, Adventure City, and Cave of Prophet Lut.

Day 13
Sea of Galilee

Sea of Galilee is called a lake of Jesus Christ. The Sea of Galilee is an attraction for Christian pilgrims who visit Israel to see the places where Jesus performed miracles according to the New Testament, such as his walking on water, calming the storm and feeding the multitude.

Day 14
Golgotha Mountain

Golgotha, or Calvary, was, according to the Gospels, a site immediately outside Jerusalem's walls where Jesus was crucified. Matthew's and Mark's gospels translate the term to mean "place of [the] skull". Then we move to Gethsemane Garden. And then move to Beersheba and stay overnight there.

Day 15, last day
Beersheba

In the Bible, Beersheba means "Well of Seven" or "Well of the Oath". Beersheba is mainly dealt with in the Hebrew Bible in connection with the Patriarchs Abraham and Isaac, who both dig a well and close peace treaties with King Abimelech of Gerar at the site. The prophet Elijah took refuge in Beersheba when Jezebel ordered him killed (I Kings 19:3).
